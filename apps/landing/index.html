03 How it works

Drop your tracks. Tell it the vibe. Hit play.

A slow LLM plans the future of the set. A fast deterministic engine owns the clock. A 30-to-60-second lookahead makes live re-prompting glitch-free.

1
Frame 01 · You

Drop your tracks

Drag a folder of your own music into the window. Each file is read on your machine for BPM, key and energy. The track rows light up as it reads them.

Analyzed locally. Nothing uploads.
2
Frame 02 · You

Describe the set

No knobs, no crates. You just talk to it. Type the night in plain language, or tap a vibe chip to nudge the mood.

Plain language in. A set comes out.
3
Frame 03 · Doremix

It conducts

An LLM writes a cue sheet; a deterministic engine plays it. Two decks, beatmatched and harmonic, riding one rising energy curve from warm-up to the drop.

A beatmatched set, built for you.
4
Frame 04 · You + Doremix

Steer it live

Mid-set, tap a quick-prompt and the curve bends to your call. A mic energy meter reads the room. The edit lands at the next phrase boundary, no dropout.

You re-steer it without missing a beat.

05 For the builders

A look at the cue sheet.

The conductor never touches a sample. It writes a bar-addressed, versioned JSON cue sheet: the contract between the LLM and the audio engine.

Typed, small, hackable. Re-prompt the set and only the future is rewritten; the playing bar is frozen. Read it, fork it, export it.
